The invisible problem

You can't avoid what you can't see

Every day, you're exposed to environmental chemicals — through the food you eat, the water you drink, the air you breathe, and the products you use. Plastics, pesticides, industrial compounds, and household chemicals accumulate silently in your body over time.

~5g of plastic per week
A WWF-commissioned study by the University of Newcastle found that people may ingest approximately 5 grams of microplastics weekly — roughly the weight of a credit card. The biggest source? Drinking water, both bottled and tap.

Comprehensive screening

27 environmental toxicants, one urine sample

Using the clinically validated TOXDetect panel by Mosaic Diagnostics, we screen for metabolites from the most common classes of environmental chemicals — the ones you encounter every single day.

Phthalates

Endocrine-disrupting chemicals found in food packaging, toys, shampoo, and vinyl flooring. Exposure is virtually universal.

MEP, MBP, MEHP, MEOHP, MiBP

Bisphenols

Used in plastics and resins — food containers, water bottles, dental sealants. "BPA-Free" alternatives like BPS may carry similar risks.

BPA, BPS

Volatile Organic Compounds

Found in household products, building materials, paints, vehicle emissions, and cigarette smoke. Easily inhaled or absorbed.

Xylene, Styrene, Benzene, Vinyl Chloride metabolites

Pesticides

Exposure through contaminated food, water, air, and soil — from agricultural and household use.

Glyphosate, Atrazine, 2,4-D, 3-PBA, DEP

Parabens

Preservatives widely used in cosmetics, skincare, and personal care products. Absorbed through the skin daily.

Methylparaben, Ethylparaben, Propylparaben, Butylparaben

Other Chemicals

Additional toxicants that contribute to chronic toxic overload through multiple exposure routes.

Oxybenzone, Perchlorate, Diphenyl Phosphate
Safety monitoring

Your liver and kidneys, closely monitored

Because the liver and kidneys are the body's primary detoxification organs, we include full clinical panels at both timepoints — reviewed by a physician — to assess toxic burden on organ function.

🫀 Liver Function Panel

  • Bilirubin
  • ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase)
  • ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase)
  • GGT (Gamma-Glutamyl Transferase)
  • Total Protein, Albumin & Globulin

🔬 Kidney Function Panel

  • Creatinine & eGFR
  • Urea & Sodium
  • Urine Microalbumin
  • Albumin:Creatinine Ratio (ACR)
  • Urine Creatinine

The process

How it works

1

Apply & Get Accepted

Fill in the form to express your interest. We'll confirm your eligibility and walk you through everything. Only 30 spots are available.

2

Baseline Testing (Day 0)

A phlebotomist visits your home for a blood draw. You also provide a urine sample using a simple collection kit. We record lifestyle variables for accuracy.

3

60-Day Study Period

Continue your daily routine without any lifestyle changes. The study evaluates changes in your environmental toxic burden over this period.

4

Daily Supplement

You'll take MasterPeace — a natural mineral supplement (zeolite + marine minerals) — each day as directed. It is non-pharmaceutical, has no known contraindications, and has been tested in published peer-reviewed studies. Learn more about it ↓

5

Follow-Up Testing (Day 60)

Repeat the same blood and urine tests. Compare results against your baseline to see how your toxic burden and organ function have changed.

6

Receive Your Full Report

Get your personal toxicology and organ function results — with clinical interpretation — giving you a detailed picture of your internal health.
